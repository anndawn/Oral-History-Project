My Research

Introduction
Research Questions

These oral history projects include the Grade 9 Oral History Project in CAPs curriculum, the Chief Albert Luthuli Oral History Competition and several oral history projects held by the District Six Museum.

I employed an ethnographic approach during one year and interviewed educators and learners about the Oral History Project.

Literature Review Interviews Participation Observation Text Analysis

This research is to analyze the influences that oral history projects have on students.

  • Will the project cultivate tolerance and respect?
  • Would the oral history project play a part in civic education?
  • Are students more likely to participate in delivering common good after the project?

    Case Analysis

    Historical Roots
    Tolerance and Respect
    Social Justice
    By interpreting the students’ work of the OHP, I argue OHP make students more aware of the historical roots of their living condition. Some township students conducting OHP discovers positive perspectives of their community. OHP also cultivates tolerance, respect and a sense of social justice in students. While tolerance and respect prepare students for demorcratic participation. The sense of justice could motivate them to participate in public affairs that could bring up social changes. These shows OHP could play a vital role in civic education.

    Intergroup Dilemma
    Dependence on Distress Cues

    Though people empathize with almost anyone in distress, they empathize more with kin, friends, and their own ethnic group (ingroup or familiarity bias). It could be a serious problem in complex societies when intergroup rivalry fosters intense empathic anger toward outgroups.
